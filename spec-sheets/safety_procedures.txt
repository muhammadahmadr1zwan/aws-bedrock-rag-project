GENERAL SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
============================

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
-----------------------------------

Mandatory PPE for All Personnel:
- Safety glasses (ANSI Z87.1 compliant) at all times in production areas
- Steel-toed safety boots (ASTM F2413 rated)
- Hard hats in designated areas (marked with signage)
- High-visibility safety vests when working near mobile equipment

Task-Specific PPE:
- Cut-resistant gloves (ANSI/ISEA cut level A4 minimum) for material handling
- Chemical-resistant gloves when handling cleaning solvents
- Hearing protection (NRR 25 dB minimum) in high-noise areas (>85 dB)
- Respiratory protection when exposure to airborne contaminants is possible
- Arc flash protective equipment when working on electrical systems

PPE Inspection and Maintenance:
- Daily inspection of all PPE before use
- Replace damaged or worn equipment immediately
- Store PPE in designated areas when not in use
- Report PPE deficiencies to supervisor immediately

MACHINE SAFETY PROCEDURES
==========================

Pre-Operation Safety Checks
---------------------------

Before Starting Any Equipment:
1. Verify all safety guards are properly installed and secured
2. Check emergency stop buttons are functional and accessible
3. Ensure work area is clear of personnel and obstacles
4. Confirm all tools and materials are properly secured
5. Review job safety analysis (JSA) for specific task
6. Verify proper PPE is worn and functional

Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) Procedures
--------------------------------

When LOTO is Required:
- Maintenance activities on energized equipment
- Cleaning inside equipment guards or enclosures
- Troubleshooting that requires guard removal
- Any non-routine activities involving potential energy exposure

LOTO Steps:
1. Notify affected personnel of planned shutdown
2. Identify all energy sources (electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, thermal)
3. Shut down equipment using normal operating procedures
4. Isolate all energy sources using designated disconnects
5. Apply personal locks and tags to isolation devices
6. Verify energy isolation by attempting normal startup
7. Release any stored energy (capacitors, springs, pressurized systems)
8. Verify zero energy state before beginning work

LOTO Removal:
1. Ensure all personnel are clear of equipment
2. Verify all tools and materials are removed
3. Reinstall guards and safety devices
4. Remove locks and tags in reverse order of application
5. Notify personnel that equipment will be re-energized
6. Test equipment operation before returning to service

Fire Emergency Procedures
-------------------------

Upon Discovery of Fire:
1. Sound fire alarm immediately
2. Attempt to extinguish ONLY if fire is small and you are trained
3. Evacuate area if fire cannot be controlled
4. Close doors behind you to contain fire spread
5. Report to designated assembly area
6. Account for all personnel in your area

Fire Extinguisher Use (PASS Method):
- Pull the pin
- Aim at base of fire
- Squeeze the handle
- Sweep from side to side

Evacuation Procedures
--------------------

Emergency Signal:
- Continuous horn blast (fire/general emergency)
- Intermittent horn blast (shelter in place)
- All clear signal: steady tone for 30 seconds

Evacuation Steps:
1. Stop work immediately and secure equipment safely
2. Assist injured or disabled personnel
3. Use nearest safe exit route (not elevators)
4. Proceed to designated assembly area
5. Report to area supervisor for headcount
6. Remain at assembly area until all-clear is given

Medical Emergency Response
-------------------------

For Serious Injuries:
1. Do not move injured person unless in immediate danger
2. Call 911 immediately
3. Notify plant medical officer
4. Provide first aid only if trained
5. Stay with injured person until help arrives
6. Secure accident scene for investigation

For Minor Injuries:
1. Report to first aid station
2. Document injury on incident report form
3. Notify supervisor within 2 hours
4. Seek medical attention if symptoms worsen

CHEMICAL SAFETY
===============

Hazardous Material Handling
---------------------------

Before Using Any Chemical:
1. Read and understand Safety Data Sheet (SDS)
2. Verify proper PPE requirements
3. Ensure adequate ventilation
4. Have spill cleanup materials available
5. Know emergency response procedures

Chemical Storage Requirements:
- Store chemicals in original containers with labels
- Segregate incompatible materials
- Maintain temperature and humidity requirements
- Use secondary containment for liquid chemicals
- Keep storage areas clean and organized

Spill Response Procedures
------------------------

Small Spills (less than 1 gallon):
1. Evacuate immediate area of non-essential personnel
2. Wear appropriate PPE before cleanup
3. Use absorbent materials from spill kit
4. Place contaminated materials in proper disposal container
5. Ventilate area until vapors dissipate
6. Report spill to supervisor

Large Spills (greater than 1 gallon):
1. Evacuate area immediately
2. Alert emergency response team
3. Prevent spread to drains or soil
4. Do not attempt cleanup without proper training
5. Ventilate area if safe to do so

ELECTRICAL SAFETY
=================

Electrical Work Requirements
---------------------------

Only Qualified Electricians May:
- Work on energized circuits above 50 volts
- Install or modify electrical systems
- Perform electrical troubleshooting
- Reset electrical protective devices

General Electrical Safety:
- Inspect electrical tools before each use
- Use GFCI protection in wet locations
- Report damaged electrical equipment immediately
- Never use electrical equipment with wet hands
- Maintain proper clearances around electrical panels

Arc Flash Protection
-------------------

Arc Flash Boundaries:
- Limited approach boundary: 3.5 feet
- Restricted approach boundary: 1 foot
- Prohibited approach boundary: Contact only by qualified persons

Required PPE for Electrical Work:
- Arc-rated clothing (minimum 8 cal/cmÂ²)
- Arc-rated face shield
- Insulated gloves rated for voltage
- Insulated tools
- Safety glasses underneath face shield

CONFINED SPACE SAFETY
=====================

Confined Space Identification
----------------------------

A confined space has:
- Limited openings for entry and exit
- Unfavorable natural ventilation
- Not designed for continuous occupancy

Examples include:
- Storage tanks and vessels
- Sewers and manholes
- Pits and sumps
- Silos and hoppers

Entry Procedures
---------------

Before Entry:
1. Obtain confined space entry permit
2. Test atmosphere for oxygen, flammable gases, and toxins
3. Ventilate space with mechanical ventilation
4. Station attendant outside space
5. Establish communication procedures
6. Have rescue equipment readily available

During Entry:
- Maintain continuous atmospheric monitoring
- Keep communication with attendant
- Exit immediately if conditions change
- Never enter to rescue without proper equipment

INCIDENT REPORTING
==================

Reportable Incidents
-------------------

All incidents must be reported, including:
- Personal injuries requiring medical attention
- Near-miss events that could have caused injury
- Property damage exceeding $500
- Environmental releases or spills
- Security breaches or threats

Reporting Timeline:
- Immediate notification for serious injuries
- Within 2 hours for all other incidents
- Written report within 24 hours

Investigation Process
--------------------

Incident Investigation Steps:
1. Secure accident scene
2. Interview witnesses separately
3. Document physical evidence
4. Identify root causes
5. Develop corrective actions
6. Follow up on implementation

Investigation focuses on:
- What happened (facts)
- Why it happened (causes)
- How to prevent recurrence (corrective actions)

COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT
==========================

Safety Performance Standards
----------------------------

Zero Tolerance for:
- Willful violation of safety procedures
- Working under the influence of drugs or alcohol
- Removal or bypassing of safety devices
- Failure to use required PPE

Progressive Discipline:
- First violation: Verbal warning and retraining
- Second violation: Written warning
- Third violation: Suspension pending investigation
- Serious violations: Immediate suspension or termination

Recognition Programs:

- Safety improvement suggestions
- Accident-free performance
- Safety leadership demonstration

Monthly Safety Awards:

- Continuous improvement initiatives
